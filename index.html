Welcome!

Welcome to Project Maverick! Our team — Jennifer Ramos, Santos Chavez, Gabriel Limones, and Logan Swagerty — worked on this project in collaboration with the UTRGV College of Engineering and Business. We hope this gives you insight into our process and results.

What Problem Are We Trying to Solve?

Construction workers in high-heat environments need improved cooling solutions to prevent heat-related illnesses and maintain comfort during long work shifts.

Important to Know

We conducted research on heat stress, current wearable cooling technologies, and workplace safety standards. Our background investigation revealed that most existing solutions are either too bulky, expensive, or ineffective for extended field use.

Why This Problem Is Important

Heat-related health risks affect thousands of outdoor workers every year. Addressing this challenge has both humanitarian and economic impact by improving safety and productivity in the construction industry.

Our Proposed Solution

We developed a wearable cooling garment that incorporates phase change materials (PCMs) and micro fans to regulate body temperature throughout the workday.

From Idea to Reality

We began by brainstorming different garment configurations, materials, and power solutions. Using CAD, we designed the structure and performed thermal simulations to optimize effectiveness. Our final concept balances weight, cost, and comfort.

Prototype Early and Often

Initial prototypes included passive cooling vests and battery-powered fan systems. These helped us gather user feedback and refine our design. We tested different fabrics, fan placements, and PCM layouts.

Final Product

Our final prototype is a modular cooling vest with integrated PCMs in high-heat zones, powered airflow vents, and a lightweight rechargeable battery pack. It is washable, ergonomic, and field-ready.

Future Work

We plan to conduct broader field trials, explore solar charging options, and evaluate commercialization pathways in the construction, agriculture, and emergency response markets.
